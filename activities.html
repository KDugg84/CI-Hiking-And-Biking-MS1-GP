The Hiking Routes

The Route Guide

Most of the routes in this guide are approved by the Galicia Mountain Federation. On the routes that have not been approved there may be few or no signs, so we recommend using a good map and/or GPS.

All the towns concils listed have undertaken to maintain these routes in good condition, so they can be used at any time of the year. The Tourism Consortium is not responsible for their maintence.

The Miño Route | Municipality of A Peroxa

Directions

The route is divided into two very different sections. The two paths that go down from A Peroxa to Gulfariz and the one that follows the banks of the River Miño from Gulfariz to Os Peares. From Ourense take the N-120 in the direction of Monforte, take the Os Peares exit and continue to the village, where the river beach is located.

Stats

  • Length: 11km
  • Difficulty: Medium
  • Duration: 3 hours 30 minutes
  • Bike Access: Unsuitable
  • Start/End Point: A Peroxa district church/River beach Os Peares
  • Services: Restaurants and accommodation in A Peroxa and Os Peares and health centre in Os Peares.

Ruta Dos Bosques Maxicos | Concello de Castro Caldelas

Directions

This route goes through some of the places of the greatest scenic, historical and ethnographic interest in the municipality of Castro Caldelas. Along the route are some very inportant historical heritage sites, including monasteries like San Pedro de Abeleda and the Poboeiros rectory, the mediaevel bridge of A Ponte and the Pozo dos Cabalos waterfall.

Stats

  • Length: 21km
  • Difficulty: Medium
  • Duration: 5 hours
  • Bike Access: Suitable
  • Start/End Point: Praza do Prado/Castro Caldelas
  • Services: Restaurants, Accommodation and Health Centre in Castro Caldelas.

The Belesar Vineyard Route | Municipality of Chantada

Directions

From Chantada take the LU-533 in the direction of Monforte. Turn off after crossing the bridge over the River Miño in the direction of Belesar. The trail continues alongside the Belesar bridge. This is an easy hike with few slopes, on which areas with vineyards alternate with chestnut and oak woods.

Stats

  • Length: 4.6km
  • Difficulty: Medium
  • Duration: 2 hours
  • Bike Access: Suitable
  • Start/End Point: Belesar/A Veiga
  • Services: Food and dring in Belesar. Accommodation and health centre in Chantada.

The San Pedro de Rocas Royal Road | Municipality of Esgos

Directions

This is one of the oldest official trails in Galicia. It goes along the old royal road that served as a link to Ourense. On this route you will find one of the most important architectural sites in Ribeira Sacra, the Monastery of San Pedro de Rocas. Take the OU-536 to Esgos, the route starts on the Praza de Souteira square by the health centre.

Stats

  • Length: 9km
  • Difficulty: Easy
  • Duration: 2 hours 45 minutes
  • Bike Access: Suitable
  • Start/End Point: Esgos
  • Services: Restaurants, health centre is Esgos.

Gabin Birch Wood Route | Municipality of Montederramo

Directions

Take the OU-536 to Leboreiro. Turn onto the OU-0602 in the direction of Montederramo. After Montederramo, take the turn-off to the right in the direction of Gabin-A Mogainza. In Mogainza, turn right onto an upaved road leading to the birch wood. There is no easy access to Campo de Casar with a vehicle.

Stats

  • Length: 9.4km
  • Difficulty: Easy
  • Duration: 3 hours
  • Bike Access: Suitable
  • Start/End Point: Campo do Casar/Serra de San Mamede
  • Services: Accommodation, restaurants and health centre in Montederramo.

The Maquino Largaño Route | Municipality of Nogueira de Ramuin

Directions

Take the OU-536. In Castadon, turn off in the direction of Luintra. The route starts at the track to the left of the new Civil Guard Barracks. The route passes through a variety of landscapes, with oak forests, birch woods, farms and villages. You can visit the Moura burial mounds, the Monastery of Santo Estevo de Ribas de Sil.

Stats

  • Length: 15km
  • Difficulty: Medium
  • Duration: 6 hours
  • Bike Access: Partially suitable
  • Start/End Point: Luintra
  • Services: Restaurants, accommodation and health centre in Luintra.

Ruta Fervenza Augacaida | Castro de Marce

Directions

The route starts on the road to Marce, on a bend with a signpost marking the start of the trail. Take a side path to get to the Aguacaida waterfall, one of the highest in Galicia. Along the trail are several remains of popular architecture, most interesting is the Castro de Marce hill fort located on top of a small hill with spectacular views of the River Miño.

Stats

  • Length: 2.2km
  • Difficulty: Easy
  • Duration: 1 hour 15 minutes
  • Bike Access: Unsuitable
  • Start/End Point: LU-P-4107/Castro de Marce
  • Services: Accommodation, restaurants and health centre in Ferreira de Panton.

The Cibrisqueiros to San Cosmede Route | Municipality of Panton

Directions

This route starts in the village of Cibrisqueiros in the parish of Pombeiro. Shortly after it starts, you can make a detour to the church of San Vicente de Pombeiro. along the way you will see the Preguntoiro Tombs, ancient anthropomorphic burial sites carved into the rock. Go through the abandoned village of Penaveada to San Cosmede, the end of the trail.

Stats

  • Length: 4km
  • Difficulty: Easy
  • Duration: 1 hour 30 minutes
  • Bike Access: Unsuitable
  • Start/End Point: Barrio de Cibrisqueiros/San Cosmede
  • Services: Restaurants, accommodation and health centre in Os Peares and Ferreira de Panton.

The River Mao Canyon Route | Municipality of Parada de Sil

Directions

From Ourense, take the OU-536 in the direction of Trives. After passing Valilongo, turn off on to the OU-139 in the direction of Cristosende. 3km from Cristosende, in the direction of Parada de Sil, is the entrance to A Fabrica da Luz; a litte further on is the turn-off for San Lourenzo de Barxacova.

Stats

  • Length: 16.5km
  • Difficulty: Medium
  • Duration: 6 hours 30 minutes
  • Bike Access: Unsuitable
  • Start/End Point: San Lourenzo de Barxacova
  • Services: Restaurants, accommodation, camping and health centre in Parade de Sil. A Fabrica de Luz hostel in Barxacova.

The Loio Route | Municipality of Paradela

Directions

Take the M-633 from Portomarin to Sarria. The trail starts by the bridge over the River Loio, in the town of Paradela, next to the municipal swimming pool. Along the way you can enjoy several waterfall, mills and a riverside wood that will offer shade the entire way. You will pass by several kinds of tracks: fisherman's tracks, dirt tracks and walled cart tracks.

Stats

  • Length: 9km
  • Difficulty: Easy
  • Duration: 2 hours 30 minutes
  • Bike Access: Suitable
  • Start/End Point: Paradela, municipal pool/Bridge over the mouth of the R. Loio
  • Services: Restaurants and health centre in Paradela.

The Gold & Iron Route | Municipality of A Pobra do Brollon

Directions

From Ourense take the N-120. 7km after Monforte de Lemos, turn right onto the LU-933 to O Empalme. From here, go in the direction of Salcedo. The route starts at the Salcedo Ethnographic Museum. Follow the course of the river to A Barxa, over the mediaeval bridge with three arches, after visiting the village of A Lama, there are remains of Roman gold mining operations.

Stats

  • Length: 11km
  • Difficulty: Easy
  • Duration: 3 hours 30 minutes
  • Bike Access: Partially suitable
  • Start/End Point: Salcedo
  • Services: Restaurants, accommodation in Salcedo and health centre in A Pobra do Brollon.

The Ferradal Trail | Municipality of Quiroga

Directions

From Ourense take the N-120. 6km from Quiroga, turn off in the direction of Soldon. On the same turn-off as for Soldon, continue on the same road to Rugando. It is an ancient road used to brig down minerals from several mines. The minerals were used in the local forges. Other places of interest, such as Pena Furada, a tunnel dug by the Romans to channel water to several gold mines.

Stats

  • Length: 11.2km
  • Difficulty: Medium/High
  • Duration: 3 hours 50 minutes
  • Bike Access: Partially suitable
  • Start/End Point: O Soldon/Ferreria de Rugando
  • Services: Restaurants, accommodation and health centre in Quiroga. Accommodation in Rugando.

The Cubela Route | Municipality of Ribas de Sil

Directions

From Ourense take the N-120 and turn right in the direction of Castro de Abaixo. This is one of the most recommended routes in the Lugo area. A circular hike that follows the bank of the Rio Sil, a major portion of the hike is along ancient paths, cut out of the rock in many places. You also have a good view of the A Cubela meander where the Rio Sil makes almost a 360 degree loop.

Stats

  • Length: 13.5km
  • Difficulty: Easy
  • Duration: 4 hours 30 minutes
  • Bike Access: Suitable
  • Start/End Point: Castro de Abaixo
  • Services: Accommodation, restaurants and health centre in San Clodio.

The Ribeira Sacra Vineyards Trail | Municipality of Sober

Directions

From Ourense take the N-120 in the direction of Monforte de Lemos and after O Castro de Ferreira turn off onto the LU-5902 to San Miguel de Rosende. After going through Rosende, continue on the same road to Os Chancis where there is a boat jetty. Landscapes on this route include the terraced vineyards along the Rio Cabe gorge, pine forests and restored water mills.

Stats

  • Length: 12.3km
  • Difficulty: Easy
  • Duration: 4 hours
  • Bike Access: Suitable
  • Start/End Point: San Miguel de Rosende/Os Chancis
  • Services: Accommodation and restaurants in San Miguel de Rosende.

The Ribeira Sacra of The Miño Route | Concellos de O Saviñao e Panton

Directions

Romanesque church of San Estevo de Ribas de Mino: Take the LU-533 from Chantada in the direction of Monforte. After 8km, there is a turn-offto the right with a signpost to the church.

  • Difficulty: Medium
  • Duration: 13 hours
  • Bike Access: Partially suitable

A Teixeira Route | Municipality of A Teixeira

Directions

From Ourense take the Ou-536 in the direction of Trives to Valilongo, and then take the Ou-0606 to A Teixeira.
On this trail we will skirt the gorges of the rivers Mao, Sil and Edo, and also cross the typical terraced vineyards of this area. The route includes the Pena do Corvo, a natural lookout point at the confluence of the rivers Sil and Edo.

Stats

  • Length: 13.7km
  • Difficulty: Medium
  • Duration: 5 hours 50 minutes
  • Bike Access: Suitable
  • Start/End Point: A Teixeira
  • Services: Accommodation and restaurants in Cristosende and A Abeleda, health centre in A Teixeira.

Long Distance & Mountain Biking Routes

The Sil Canyon

Directions

The Sil Canyon Route is a circular route that uses parts of other, shorter trails and, by linking them together, runs along both sides of the Sil Canyon, between San Clodio and Os Peares for 200km. As this is a circular route, you can start anywhere, but it is advisable to start oit in one of the large population centres, such as Os Peares, Castro Caldelas, Parada de Sil, Doade, Aguasmestas.

Stats

Along this route you will enjoy the landscape of the canyon, Romanesque architecture, cross woods of great ecological value and visit a number of villages. Along the way there is rural accommodation, where you can spend the night, get back your strengh and enjoy the good food and wine of the area.

The Miño Gorge

Directions

This circular route uses both banks of the river Miño, taking tou from the Os Peares dam to the outskirts of Taboada. It goes through the municipalities of Panton, O Saviñao, Taboada, Chantada and Carballedo. Following various hiking routes. forestry roads and Roman roads, you will go the entire length of the Miño Gorge.

Stats

Along the picturesque landscapes and Romanesque art, you will fin the impressive Aguacaida waterfall, the Codos of Belesar Roman Road, the Monastery of San Estevo de Ribas de Miño and the church of Santa Maria de Pesqueiras.

The Ribeira Sacra Mountain Biking Centre

Directions

The Ribeira Sacra Mountain Biking Centre is to the south of the Sil gorge, on the border between the provinces of Lugo and Ourense. Five circular routes, totaling 110km, lets you discover the municipalities of Nogueira de Ramuin and Parade de Sil. These rides have different levels of difficulty, although the tough local terrain makes most of them demanding.

Stats
